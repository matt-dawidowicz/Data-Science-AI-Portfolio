Citi Bike Time Series Profile

Trip-start demand profile for Citi Bike, January 2024. Source: Citi Bike public trip-history archive.

Executive Summary

  • The dataset is portfolio-scale but still practical. The profile uses 1,886,109 valid rides across 744 hourly observations after filtering implausible trip durations.
  • The series has a strong human rhythm. Rush-hour demand averaged 4,328 starts per hour, 2.2x off-hour demand, and the weekday/hour heatmap exposes commute-heavy peaks.
  • A basic seasonal forecast is already useful. The best baseline on the Jan. 25-31 holdout was calendar_profile, with MAE of 796 rides per hour and RMSE of 1,075.

Demand shape is the main story, not the model name

A strong showcase should first prove the temporal structure. This month has enough daily and hourly observations to show trend, weekday rhythm, commute peaks, holiday effects, station concentration, and forecast error. That gives the project a real analytical spine before introducing heavier forecasting methods.

Seasonality is visible at commuter granularity

The weekday/hour profile is the most useful first feature view. It shows whether the model should expect a commute pattern, leisure pattern, or irregular event-driven pattern. Here, the rush-hour average is materially higher than off-hours, and weekends average 0.71x weekday hourly volume.

Stations and anomalies create the portfolio hooks

Operational texture makes the showcase feel real. The top start station was W 21 St & 6 Ave, with 8,325 starts. The largest anomaly flagged in this pass occurred at 2024-01-18 12:00:00, when actual starts differed from the weekday/hour profile by -1,430 rides.

Recommended Next Steps

  1. Extend the pull to 12-24 months so yearly seasonality, weather sensitivity, and regime changes are visible.
  2. Add station-cluster forecasts for the top 50 stations or neighborhoods. That turns the project into a practical allocation problem.
  3. Fit one stronger model after the baselines: SARIMAX with calendar/weather features, or a gradient-boosted lag-feature model.

Further Questions

Which business decision should the forecast serve: staffing, rebalancing, rider communications, station planning, or anomaly alerting? The answer determines the forecast horizon, error metric, and acceptable tradeoff between aggregate accuracy and station-level specificity.

Caveats and Assumptions

This is a first-pass profile on one winter month, not a production forecast. It intentionally emphasizes clean temporal profiling and transparent baselines. Open-Meteo hourly weather joined successfully for optional context. For a full showcase, extend the history and validate across multiple rolling holdout windows.

Public sources used: Citi Bike trip history archive and, when available, Open-Meteo historical weather. Generated outputs include hourly profiles, backtest metrics, anomaly tables, and chart PNGs in this folder.
